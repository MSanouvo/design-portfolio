Background

For a prototyping class I took during my senior year of undergrad (DSGN 100) I was assigned to develop an app that would aid student learning during the COVID-19 Pandemic.

Through interviewing stakeholders and doing user research, my team and I decided on motivating students with a platform to work collaboratively with their classmates as they were suddenly transitioning to online education.

User Research

We started our research by searching through multiple online groups and forums, observing the different discussions and opinions on the shift towards online education. Once we had a consensus on what potential problems we wanted to address, my team and I created a questionnaire to gather more thoughts in regards to those problems we found to be prevalent among stakeholders. The questionnaire would address the academic lives that students lived before and after the pandemic began to discover if there was a change in their learning and if there was, how so.

User Personas

Using the information gathered from our user research. our team drafted two personas that we thought would accurately relate to the target stakeholders of our product.

We portrayed Zach as an incoming college freshman, excited about making friends and memories during his time on campus. With the pandemic affecting in person classes and events around campus, students like Zach found difficulty in connecting with people and making those memories they desire.

Zach prefers studying with friends and finds it difficult to do so while his education is being moved to remote learning.

We portrayed Julia as a grad student with a lot of work to manage. She’s the type that seeks to maintain close connections with her professors and advisors in order to help her succeed with their guidance and advice. She is also particular about her study habits and prefers to get out of her room and study in an open and relaxing environment such as a café or library.

Due to the pandemic, Julia has had issues connecting with her advisors and finding a suitable way of studying from her home.

Storyboards

As a team, we produced individual storyboards that would describe different scenarios that our stakeholders would go through that our app aids in to help their experience. We thought of different situations and envisioned how our app would help stakeholders with their given situation.

Sketeches and Wireframing

Once the team had a solid idea of what we wanted with our app, we began sketching out the aspects that we envisioned during our storyboard.

Using figma, we worked on separate sections of the app that coincide with the features introduced during our storyboarding. With each individual piece created, we put the low fidelity wireframe together to see where each piece would connect and how.

Prototyping

With a wireframe made, we worked on producing a high fidelity prototype by incorporating a style guide and updating our past iterations with a better look.

Reflection

While my group and I were able to progress through the early researching phase to a high fidelity prototype in the span of 6 weeks for this project, I still feel that there was more that could've been done for our product.

In many cases, I would say I chose to settle on some aspects of our work. Perhaps it was due to the short time span and my adjustment to online learning, but there were instances where I felt better ideas and designs could have been iterated on given more time on some stages in our project. Specifically for parts such as our personas, I believe that I could have better depicted accurate stakeholders that feel more realistic to the data found during our user research. While Zach fell more in line with traits obtained from our research, I feel that the depiction of Julia isn't entirely accurate with the stakeholders we were targeting.

Even before our personas, I thought that our user research questionnaire may not have been as organized and thought out as it could have been. Some questions felt redundant but I didn't speak up on those issues due to not thinking of better alternatives myself.

I believe many of these issues were the culprit of lack of brainstorming, leaving me to settle on the current ideas generated whilst the possibility of better ideas were never explored. Given the short time frame we were given for these stages of the project, utilizing more rapid brainstorming methods would've greatly aided my creative thinking on core stages of the project.
